Realistic Research: University, Web-based, and Local Resources

Overview

This lesson is designed for students starting their research journey in their first semester of the TCID, IA, or Ph.D. in Technical Communication program. It aims to help students develop, improve, or refresh their research skills. The lesson provides strategies to make research more efficient.

Content Areas

1. Getting Started

  • Accessing IIT Library Services
    • Learn how to access and navigate IIT library services.
  • Basic Functionality on the IIT Library Website
    • Understand how to use the main features of the IIT library website for research purposes.

2. Research Timelines

  • Formulating a Timeline
    • Learn how to create an effective research timeline to guide your work.
  • Using the Timeline for Resource Acquisition
    • Discover how to use your timeline to plan when and how to gather the necessary research resources.

3. Requesting Resources

  • Requesting Articles from Other Libraries
    • Learn the process of requesting journal articles and other resources from external libraries.
  • Requesting Books from Other Libraries
    • Understand how to request books from libraries beyond IIT.

4. Local Libraries

  • Accessing Local Libraries
    • Learn how to make use of nearby local libraries for research materials.
  • When to Utilize Local Libraries vs. Interlibrary Loan
    • Learn the factors that determine whether to visit a local library or request materials through interlibrary loan services.

5. Finding Resources on the Web

  • Web-Based Research Tools
    • Review various online research tools and strategies for gathering resources from the web.

Quiz

After reading the sections, you will complete a short quiz to help reflect on the strategies and tools presented in this lesson. The quiz will reinforce the skills learned and assist you in applying them to your research tasks.
